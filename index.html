About Me



After years of honorably serving in the US Armed Forces (Active and National Guard), I decided that jumping out of airplanes while exhilarating wasn’t something I wanted to do for the rest of my life. What's funny about it, I came to the conclusion reading from an Army Field manual describing parachute specifications. I noticed the care to detail, the data tables, the diagrams and schemas. I love details, but even more than that, I love the power of details.

Packing parachutes required endless details, from tensile strength to rigorous attention to procedure, and when accomplished successfully, you saved lives every day. I decided going to college and getting a degree in a technical field was the best path for me, so I could continue to be impactful with the details in a modern technological world. I began my journey in 2014 at the College of Engineering at Tennessee State University for my degree in Computer Science, graduated in the fall of 2017.

Now, I’m being impactful in another way. Working with others in developing projects has granted me more opportunities with ever greater responsibilities. I delight in being of value, always seek to improve myself and be an asset wherever I am. Technology and the rate of advancement gives me endless opportunities to challenges. I thrive in rigorous environments — I love to run most days, and traveling abroad whenever I get the chance. In my spare time, I teach myself French.
